Assam - Khaar 🍛


Ingredients for Assamese Khaar 🍲

  • 1/2 Cup Raw Papaya (peeled and chopped) 🥭
  • 1/2 Cup Masoor Dal (red lentils) 🥣
  • 2-3 Green Chilies (slit) 🌶️
  • 1 tbsp Khaar (alkaline water from filtered ash of certain plants)* 🌱
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder ✨
  • 1 tbsp Mustard Oil 🛢️
  • 1/2 tsp Cumin Seeds 🌱
  • 1-2 Bay Leaves 🍃
  • 1-2 Cloves 🌿
  • 1 Cinnamon Stick 🍂
  • Salt to taste 🧂
  • Fresh Coriander for garnish 🌿
  • 4-5 Cups Water 💧

Steps 🍴

  1. In a pan, heat mustard oil over medium heat. Add cumin seeds, bay leaves, cloves, and cinnamon stick. Let them splutter for a few seconds. 🌱🍃
  2. Add chopped raw papaya and sauté for 2-3 minutes. 🥭
  3. Add turmeric powder and salt, then mix well. ✨🧂
  4. Add red lentils (masoor dal) to the pan and mix everything together. 🥣
  5. Pour in 4-5 cups of water and bring it to a boil. Once it starts boiling, reduce the heat and simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the papaya and lentils are cooked soft. 💧
  6. Once the lentils and papaya are cooked, add the **khaar** (alkaline water strained from the ashes of certain plants, usually from the “Sakua” plant). Stir well to mix it in. 🌱
  7. Simmer the dish for another 5-7 minutes to allow the flavors to blend together. Adjust salt if needed. 🧂
  8. Add the slit green chilies for an extra kick, and garnish with fresh coriander leaves before serving. 🌿🌶️
