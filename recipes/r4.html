Sarson da Saag & Makki di Roti! 🌽🌿


Ingredients for Sarson da Saag & Makki di Roti 🌿🍞

Sarson da Saag 🌿

  • 500g Mustard Leaves (Sarson) 🌿
  • 200g Spinach (Palak) 🌱
  • 100g Bathua Leaves (optional) 🌿
  • 1 Onion (finely chopped) 🧅
  • 2 Tomatoes (chopped) 🍅
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste 🧄
  • 2-3 Green Chilies (slit) 🌶️
  • 1 tbsp Red Chili Powder 🌶️
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder ✨
  • 1 tsp Garam Masala 🌿
  • 1/2 tsp Asafoetida (Hing) 🧂
  • 1/4 Cup Makki Flour (cornmeal) 🌽
  • 2 tbsp Ghee (clarified butter) or Oil 🍯
  • Salt to taste 🧂

Makki di Roti 🍞

  • 2 Cups Makki (cornmeal) 🌽
  • Warm Water as needed 💧
  • Salt to taste 🧂
  • Ghee or Butter for cooking 🍯🧈

Steps 🍴

Sarson da Saag 🌿

  1. Wash the mustard leaves, spinach, and bathua leaves (if using) thoroughly. Chop them finely. 🌿
  2. In a large pan, heat some ghee or oil. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown. 🧅
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for 2 minutes until fragrant. 🧄
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften. 🍅
  5. Add red chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, and asafoetida. Stir well and cook for 2 minutes. 🌶️✨🌿
  6. Add the chopped mustard leaves, spinach, and bathua leaves. Stir to mix with the spices. 🌱🌿
  7. Add a little water and cook the greens on medium heat for 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally. 🌿
  8. After the leaves are cooked and softened, use a hand blender or masher to mash them slightly to make them creamy. 🌿
  9. Mix in the makki flour (cornmeal) and continue cooking for another 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to blend. 🌽
  10. Adjust salt to taste and drizzle ghee over the saag before serving. 🍯

Makki di Roti 🍞

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the makki flour and salt. Add warm water gradually and knead into a smooth dough. 🌽💧
  2. Divide the dough into small balls (about the size of a golf ball). 🏐
  3. Place a dough ball between two sheets of parchment paper or a plastic sheet. Gently roll it out into a circular shape using your fingers or a rolling pin. 🌽
  4. Heat a tawa (griddle) on medium heat and cook the roti on both sides until golden brown. Brush with ghee or butter on both sides while cooking. 🍯🧈
  5. Serve the hot Makki di Roti with Sarson da Saag and enjoy! 🍞🌿
